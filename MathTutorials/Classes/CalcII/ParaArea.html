The cycloid represents the following situation. Consider a wheel of radius r.  Let the point where the wheel touches the ground initially be called P.  Then start rolling the wheel to the right.  As the wheel rolls to the right trace out the path of the point P.  The path that the point P traces out is called a cycloid and is given by the equations above.  In these equations we can think of θ as the angle through which the point P has rotated.
